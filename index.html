Cover

The clasps are black iron, cold to the touch. Leather creaks as the spine breathes. The page edges are dark with travel.

Ink stains the margin where a hand once trembled. Fibers in the parchment rise and fall like tide under skin.

Nothing watches. Everything listens.

Frontispiece

The Chronicle records those who walk between storms. Names etched here rarely survive the retelling, yet the path requires them still.

Open gently; the bindings remember strain. The words will find their places as you go.

Preface

This bound tome gathers strands from sea-salt wake and fire-lit anvil, from high cloud and stone-root. The script is patient. The reader, moreso.
